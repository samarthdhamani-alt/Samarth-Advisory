Questions

Things business owners actually ask.

What documents do I need to share?

Three years of profit-and-loss statements, a rough monthly breakdown of owner drawings or salary, your current lease, and an approximate employee count and payroll figure. If your books aren't formal, hand-written ledgers work — I'll structure them.

Will my financial information stay confidential?

Yes. I'll sign an NDA on the spot if you ask. Any case-study reference in my university application would be anonymised — the business identified only as, for example, "a Jumeirah-based F&B operator with AED 2.5m annual revenue."

What if I'm not sure my financials are clean enough?

Most SMEs don't have clean books — that's normal. I work with what you have, flag where assumptions had to be made, and you decide whether the conclusions hold. Imperfect data is still useful data.

Are you trying to sell me something later?

No. There's no follow-up product, no subscription, no referral fee from a broker. The report is the whole thing. If we both find the work useful, you might recommend me to another business owner — but you're not obligated to.

Why should I trust analysis from a teenager?

You shouldn't trust me on faith. Trust the methodology. The valuation methods are industry-standard and verifiable. The report cites every assumption. If you take it to a licensed advisor afterwards and they think the conclusions are wrong, you've still paid nothing and lost nothing but a few hours.
